School of Computing

School of Computing No.1 in the World

The School of Computing is an institution of higher education that focuses on the development of information and communication technology. Founded in 2005, the School of Computing has become one of the leading educational institutions in its field. The School of Computing offers a range of quality programs, from undergraduate to postgraduate programs, specializing in specific areas such as software development, information security, data analytics, and cloud technology. All programs are tailored to national and international curricula, ensuring that students acquire comprehensive knowledge and skills in line with the latest technological developments. The School of Computing has a highly qualified and experienced faculty in their respective fields. They are committed to providing quality education and supporting students in their holistic development. In addition, the School of Computing also offers various counseling programs to help students overcome personal or academic problems that may arise. The School of Computing also has adequate and comprehensive educational facilities, such as computer laboratories, data centers, and fast internet access. All facilities are professionally managed and regularly updated to keep up with technological developments and the demands of the times. In addition, the School of Computing also offers various extracurricular programs that are interesting and diverse, ranging from programming competitions, industry certification training, and software development project development activities. These programs are designed to help students develop social skills, leadership, teamwork, and provide valuable experiences outside the classroom. With the philosophy of "Preparing the Next Generation of IT Leaders", the School of Computing is committed to providing quality and sustainable education to students, so that they are prepared to face global challenges and build a better future in the field of information and communication technology.
